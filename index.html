CODING AT IYF

WHY I LOVE CODING.

One could love coding because it gives you the power to turn ideas into real, working things using just logic and creativity. It’s like solving puzzles where you’re constantly figuring out how to make something work, fix errors, and improve it until it feels right. Coding also gives instant feedback — you write something, run it, and immediately see results, which makes learning very interactive and rewarding. On top of that, it’s a skill that builds real-world impact, whether you’re making websites, apps, games, or tools that other people use. The more you learn, the more control you feel over technology instead of just consuming it, and that sense of creation and problem-solving is what makes many people genuinely enjoy it.

CODING: THE COMPUTERS LANGUAGE

Computers don’t understand human language directly — they only understand very strict instructions written in programming languages like JavaScript, Python, or C++. So when you code, you’re basically acting as a translator between human ideas and the computer’s logic. You break down what you want into precise steps the computer can follow, almost like giving it a recipe with no room for confusion. That’s why coding feels like “talking to a machine in its language” — but really, it’s you learning the structured languages humans created so we can communicate with computers effectively.

THE NEED FOR COMPUTER PROGRAMMERS.

The modern world needs programmers because almost everything around us runs on software. From phones, websites, apps, and social media to banking systems, transport, healthcare, and even home appliances, programmers are the people who build and maintain the systems that keep all of it working. They turn real-world problems into digital solutions, making life faster, more connected, and more efficient. Without programmers, technology wouldn’t improve or even function properly because every update, security system, and new feature depends on code being written, tested, and maintained. As the world becomes more digital, programmers are basically the people who keep the modern world running and evolving.
